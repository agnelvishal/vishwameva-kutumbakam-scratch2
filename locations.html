Dream Locations

Journey through our curated selection of extraordinary destinations around the world. Each location has been chosen for its cultural significance, natural beauty, and potential to nurture community and spiritual growth.

Explore our dream locations around the world where we envision creating harmonious Zen Abodes. Each location has been carefully selected for its unique cultural significance, natural beauty, and potential to foster community and spiritual growth.

Auroville, India

Auroville is an experimental township in Viluppuram district, mostly in the state of Tamil Nadu, India, with some parts in the Union Territory of Pondicherry in India. It was founded in 1968 by Mirra Alfassa and designed by architect Roger Anger. Auroville – an ideal township devoted to an experiment in human unity.

Northern Light - Akureyri, Iceland

Akureyri, Iceland's charming northern gem, nestles by Eyjafjörður, boasting majestic mountain backdrops. Known as the "Capital of the North," it offers vibrant culture, including the iconic Akureyri Church and serene Botanical Garden. Winter brings snow adventures, while summer illuminates stunning landscapes under the midnight sun, inviting exploration.

Learn More

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal, India's architectural masterpiece in Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this iconic ivory-white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love, featuring intricate craftsmanship and symmetrical beauty.

Learn More

Colosseum, Rome

The Colosseum in Rome, Italy, an ancient amphitheater built in 70-80 AD, is an iconic symbol of Roman engineering and entertainment. Hosting gladiator contests and spectacles for thousands, it stands as a monumental testament to Roman history, architectural prowess, and cultural heritage.

Learn More

Victoria Falls, Zambia

Victoria Falls, located on the Zambezi River, defines the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe. Known as "The Smoke That Thunders," it's one of the world's largest waterfalls, captivating with its thunderous roar, vast cascades, and a mesmerizing curtain of mist, offering a breathtaking natural spectacle.

Learn More

Parícutin Volcano, Mexico

Parícutin Volcano in Mexico emerged suddenly in 1943 from a farmer's field, growing to over 1,300 feet. Its eruption was witnessed, and its creation studied, making it a unique scientific landmark. Parícutin's rapid formation is a remarkable testament to nature's unpredictable forces.

Learn More

Amazon River, Ecuador

The Amazon River in Ecuador flows through dense rainforests, teeming with unparalleled biodiversity. This vital artery of the Amazon Basin sustains vibrant ecosystems, offering boat excursions for wildlife spotting and glimpses of indigenous cultures. Its waters and surrounding jungles harbor diverse species and unique ecological treasures.

Learn More

Komodo Island, Indonesia

Komodo Island in Indonesia is famed for its iconic Komodo dragons, the world's largest lizards. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers rugged landscapes, pink sand beaches, and unparalleled biodiversity. Visitors encounter these prehistoric creatures in their natural habitat, making it a unique wildlife destination.

Learn More

Inuit, Canada

The Inuit, indigenous peoples of Canada's Arctic regions, inhabit vast territories spanning from Alaska to Greenland. Renowned for their rich cultural heritage, traditional hunting, and unique adaptations to harsh environments, they possess deep-rooted knowledge of survival, art, storytelling, and spirituality, preserving their distinct way of life.

Learn More

Mystery Location

Nestled amidst the vast expanse of Iceland, the Blue Lagoon is a geothermal oasis offering a surreal experience. Its milky blue waters, rich in minerals, create a rejuvenating spa haven. Surrounded by volcanic landscapes and steamy waters, it's a serene escape that merges natural beauty with therapeutic relaxation.

Learn More

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu, Peru, nestled in the Andes Mountains, is an ancient Incan citadel renowned for its mystical allure and breathtaking scenery. This UNESCO World Heritage site captivates with its impressive stone architecture, mysterious ruins, and panoramic views, offering a window into the rich history and cultural legacy of the Inca civilization.

Learn More

Petra, Jordan

Petra, Jordan, an ancient city carved into rose-red cliffs, captivates with its archaeological marvels. Built by the Nabateans around 300 BCE, it boasts iconic structures like the Treasury and Monastery, blending natural beauty with stunning rock-cut architecture, making it a UNESCO World Heritage site and cultural treasure.

Learn More

Welcoming Christ & Sugar Loaf, Rio, Brazil

The Welcoming Christ statue atop Corcovado Mountain and the Sugarloaf Mountain in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, offer stunning vistas. The iconic Christ the Redeemer, symbolizing peace, overlooks the city, while the cable car journey to Sugarloaf presents panoramic views of Rio's coastline, blending natural beauty and cultural landmarks.

Learn More

Grand Canyon, USA

The Grand Canyon, a colossal geological wonder in Arizona, USA, stretches for 277 miles, carved by the Colorado River. Its sheer size, layered rock formations, and vibrant hues unveil millions of years of Earth's history, attracting visitors worldwide to behold its breathtaking vistas and awe-inspiring natural beauty.

Learn More

Iguazu Falls, Argentina

Iguazu Falls, bordering Argentina and Brazil, is a stunning natural wonder comprising over 250 individual cascades. The thundering waters plunge from cliffs amidst lush rainforest, creating a mesmerizing spectacle. This UNESCO World Heritage site captivates visitors with its sheer grandeur and breathtaking beauty.

Learn More

Halong Bay, Vietnam

Halong Bay in Vietnam is a UNESCO World Heritage site famed for its emerald waters and thousands of towering limestone islands topped with lush vegetation. This enchanting seascape, adorned with caves and grottoes, offers stunning boat cruises amidst its breathtaking scenery, attracting visitors worldwide.

Learn More

Puerto Princesa Underground River, Philippines

The Puerto Princesa Underground River in the Philippines is a UNESCO-listed site renowned for its navigable subterranean river flowing through a limestone cave. This natural wonder, boasting diverse ecosystems and impressive rock formations, offers mesmerizing boat tours amidst its enchanting and ecologically rich underground landscapes.

Learn More

Pyramids of Egypt

The Pyramids of Egypt, including the Great Pyramid of Giza, are ancient monumental tombs built for pharaohs. Symbolizing architectural marvels, they stand as enduring symbols of Egypt's rich history and craftsmanship, showcasing ancient engineering brilliance and captivating visitors with their grandeur and enigmatic allure.

Learn More

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, Cambodia's ancient marvel, stands as the world's largest religious monument. A testament to Khmer architecture, its intricate sandstone temples and ornate bas-reliefs narrate tales of ancient empire. Majestic spires pierce the sky in this UNESCO heritage site, drawing visitors into its mystical historical embrace.

Learn More

Great Wall Of China

The Great Wall of China, an ancient marvel stretching over 13,000 miles, is an iconic symbol of Chinese history. Built over centuries of dynasties, this fortification protected against invasions. Its architectural grandeur, cultural significance, and panoramic vistas make it a UNESCO World Heritage site and a global wonder.

Learn More

Chichén Itzá, Mexico

Chichén Itzá, in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, is an ancient Mayan city famed for its iconic pyramid, El Castillo. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it showcases remarkable Mayan architecture, including the Temple of the Warriors and the observatory El Caracol, reflecting celestial knowledge and cultural significance.

Learn More

Mount Everest, Nepal

Mount Everest, Earth's highest peak at 29,032 feet, resides in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. A challenging pinnacle for climbers, it symbolizes nature's magnificence. Everest's allure captivates adventurers, offering a majestic yet perilous journey amidst awe-inspiring landscapes and extreme altitudes.

Learn More

Great Barrier Reef, Australia

The Great Barrier Reef, off Australia's coast, is the world's largest coral reef system. Spanning over 1,400 miles, it teems with vibrant marine life, comprising thousands of coral reefs and islands. This UNESCO World Heritage site captivates with its biodiversity, offering unparalleled snorkeling, diving, and ecological wonders.

Learn More

Table Mountain, South Africa

Table Mountain in South Africa, overlooking Cape Town, is an iconic landmark with a flat-topped summit. Offering panoramic views of the city and ocean, it entices visitors with diverse flora, hiking trails, and a cableway for a scenic ascent. This natural wonder is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Learn More

Jeju Island, South Korea

Jeju Island, South Korea, is a volcanic paradise known for its stunning landscapes, including Hallasan Mountain, lava tubes, waterfalls, and scenic coastlines. This UNESCO World Heritage site boasts unique geological features, vibrant culture, and attractions like the Loveland sculpture park, drawing visitors to its natural beauty and cultural richness.

Learn More

Queenstown, New Zealand

Queenstown in New Zealand is an adventurer's paradise nestled by Lake Wakatipu and the Southern Alps. Renowned for thrilling activities like bungee jumping, skiing, and skydiving, this picturesque town offers stunning landscapes, vibrant nightlife, and a wealth of outdoor experiences, making it a magnet for adrenaline enthusiasts.

Learn More

Jerusalem, Israel

Jerusalem is an ancient city in the Middle East, considered holy to three major Abrahamic religions—Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. With a history spanning thousands of years, it features iconic sites such as the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Dome of the Rock.
