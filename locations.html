Dream Locations

Explore our dream locations around the world where we envision creating harmonious Zen Abodes. Each location has been carefully selected for its unique cultural significance, natural beauty, and potential to foster community and spiritual growth.

Auroville, India

Auroville is an experimental township in Viluppuram district, mostly in the state of Tamil Nadu, India, with some parts in the Union Territory of Pondicherry in India. It was founded in 1968 by Mirra Alfassa and designed by architect Roger Anger. Auroville – an ideal township devoted to an experiment in human unity.

Northern Light - Akureyri, Iceland

Akureyri, Iceland's charming northern gem, nestles by Eyjafjörður, boasting majestic mountain backdrops. Known as the "Capital of the North," it offers vibrant culture, including the iconic Akureyri Church and serene Botanical Garden. Winter brings snow adventures, while summer illuminates stunning landscapes under the midnight sun, inviting exploration.

Learn More

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal, India's architectural masterpiece in Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this iconic ivory-white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love, featuring intricate craftsmanship and symmetrical beauty.

Learn More

Colosseum, Rome

The Colosseum in Rome, an ancient amphitheater and UNESCO World Heritage site, is a symbol of Roman engineering and history. Built in the 1st century AD, this iconic structure once hosted gladiator contests and public spectacles, standing as a monumental testament to Italy's imperial past.

Learn More

Victoria Falls, Zambia

Victoria Falls, on the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe, is one of the world's largest waterfalls and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Known locally as Mosi-oa-Tunya ('The Smoke That Thunders'), it's a breathtaking natural wonder characterized by its immense width and dramatic cascade into the Zambezi River.

Learn More

Parícutin Volcano, Mexico

Parícutin Volcano in Mexico is a cinder cone volcano that gained fame for its rapid birth in a farmer's cornfield in 1943. As one of the youngest volcanoes in the world, it offers a unique and accessible site for witnessing the impact of volcanic activity on the landscape, marking it as a significant geological phenomenon.

Learn More

Amazon River, Ecuador

The Amazon River in Ecuador flows through dense rainforests, teeming with unparalleled biodiversity. This vital artery of the Amazon Basin sustains vibrant ecosystems, offering boat excursions for wildlife spotting and glimpses of indigenous cultures. Its waters and surrounding jungles harbor diverse species and unique ecological treasures.

Learn More

Komodo Island, Indonesia

Komodo Island in Indonesia is famed for its iconic Komodo dragons, the world's largest lizards. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers rugged landscapes, pink sand beaches, and unparalleled biodiversity. Visitors encounter these prehistoric creatures in their natural habitat, making it a unique wildlife destination.

Learn More

Inuit, Canada

Experience the distinctive culture and resilience of the Inuit people in Canada's Arctic regions. From traditional art and storytelling to modern sustainable living, the Inuit communities offer unique insights into life in one of the world's most extreme environments.

Learn More

Mystery Location

Escape to the captivating and geologically active Blue Lagoon in Iceland. Surrounded by otherworldly landscapes, this iconic destination invites you to unwind in mineral-rich geothermal waters while experiencing the unique beauty of nature's thermal wonders.

Learn More

Machu Picchu, Peru

Perched high in the Andes Mountains of Peru, Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca citadel of breathtaking beauty. This UNESCO World Heritage site captures the imagination with its enigmatic ruins, terraced landscapes, and spiritual aura, offering a glimpse into the ingenuity of Inca civilization.

Learn More

Petra, Jordan

Petra, Jordan, an ancient city carved into rose-red cliffs, captivates with its archaeological marvels. Built by the Nabateans around 300 BCE, it boasts iconic structures like the Treasury and Monastery, blending natural beauty with stunning rock-cut architecture, making it a UNESCO World Heritage site and cultural treasure.

Learn More

Welcoming Christ & Sugar Loaf, Rio, Brazil

Ascend to the summit of Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, where the iconic Christ the Redeemer statue welcomes you. Nearby, Sugar Loaf Mountain offers panoramic views of the vibrant city, lush forests, and the sparkling Atlantic Ocean.

Learn More

Grand Canyon, USA

Behold the awe-inspiring beauty of the Grand Canyon, a geological masterpiece carved by the Colorado River in the United States. Its vast and colorful expanse of deep canyons and towering cliffs offers a profound connection to the Earth's ancient history.

Learn More

Iguazu Falls, Argentina

Witness the sheer power of Iguazu Falls, a breathtaking sequence of waterfalls on the border of Argentina and Brazil. Surrounded by lush subtropical rainforests, this UNESCO World Heritage site creates a mesmerizing spectacle of cascading water and vibrant biodiversity.

Learn More

Halong Bay, Vietnam

Sail through Halong Bay, Vietnam, where emerald waters are dotted with thousands of towering limestone karsts. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers a mystical landscape of caves, secluded beaches, and traditional floating villages, creating a truly enchanting maritime experience.

Learn More

Puerto Princesa Underground River, Philippines

Explore the subterranean wonders of the Puerto Princesa Underground River in the Philippines. This New7Wonders of Nature site features a navigable underground river winding through limestone caves, revealing a breathtaking world of stalactites and stalagmites.

Learn More

Pyramids of Egypt

The Giza Pyramid Complex, an archaeological site on the Giza Plateau, on the outskirts of Cairo, Egypt, includes the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of Ancient Egypt.

Learn More

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, Cambodia's ancient marvel, stands as the world's largest religious monument. A testament to Khmer architecture, its intricate sandstone temples and ornate bas-reliefs narrate tales of ancient empire. Majestic spires pierce the sky in this UNESCO heritage site, drawing visitors into its mystical historical embrace.

Learn More

Great Wall Of China

The Great Wall of China, an ancient marvel stretching over 13,000 miles, is an iconic symbol of Chinese history. Built over centuries of dynasties, this fortification protected against invasions. Its architectural grandeur, cultural significance, and panoramic vistas make it a UNESCO World Heritage site and a global wonder.

Learn More

Chichén Itzá, Mexico

Chichén Itzá, in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, is an ancient Mayan city famed for its iconic pyramid, El Castillo. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it showcases remarkable Mayan architecture, including the Temple of the Warriors and the observatory El Caracol, reflecting celestial knowledge and cultural significance.

Learn More

Mount Everest, Nepal

Mount Everest, Earth's highest peak at 29,032 feet, resides in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. A challenging pinnacle for climbers, it symbolizes nature's magnificence. Everest's allure captivates adventurers, offering a majestic yet perilous journey amidst awe-inspiring landscapes and extreme altitudes.

Learn More

Great Barrier Reef, Australia

The Great Barrier Reef, off Australia's coast, is the world's largest coral reef system. Spanning over 1,400 miles, it teems with vibrant marine life, comprising thousands of coral reefs and islands. This UNESCO World Heritage site captivates with its biodiversity, offering unparalleled snorkeling, diving, and ecological wonders.

Learn More

Table Mountain, South Africa

Table Mountain in South Africa, overlooking Cape Town, is an iconic landmark with a flat-topped summit. Offering panoramic views of the city and ocean, it entices visitors with diverse flora, hiking trails, and a cableway for a scenic ascent. This natural wonder is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Learn More

Jeju Island, South Korea

Discover Jeju Island, South Korea's largest island, known for its volcanic landscapes, scenic beaches, and unique cultural heritage. From lush mountains to pristine shores, it offers a blend of natural beauty and adventure for all travelers.

Learn More

Queenstown, New Zealand

Experience Queenstown, New Zealand, the adventure capital of the world, offering stunning alpine scenery and a wide range of outdoor activities. Whether you seek thrills or tranquility, Queenstown beckons with its breathtaking natural wonders.

Learn More

Jerusalem, Israel

Jerusalem is an ancient city in the Middle East, considered holy to three major Abrahamic religions—Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. With a history spanning thousands of years, it features iconic sites such as the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Dome of the Rock.
